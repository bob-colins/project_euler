# [Product-sum numbers](http://projecteuler.net/problem=88)

A natural number, N, that can be written as the sum and product of a given set of at least two natural numbers, {_a_<sub>1</sub>, _a_<sub>2</sub>, ... , _a_<sub><i>k</i></sub>} is called a product-sum number: N = _a_<sub>1</sub> + _a_<sub>2</sub> + ... + _a_<sub><i>k</i></sub> = _a_<sub>1</sub> ![×](/Volumes/HDD_KS/source/project_euler/vender/bundle/ruby/2.2.0/gems/euler-manager-0.1.1/config/../data/images/symbol_times.gif) _a_<sub>2</sub> ![×](/Volumes/HDD_KS/source/project_euler/vender/bundle/ruby/2.2.0/gems/euler-manager-0.1.1/config/../data/images/symbol_times.gif) ... ![×](/Volumes/HDD_KS/source/project_euler/vender/bundle/ruby/2.2.0/gems/euler-manager-0.1.1/config/../data/images/symbol_times.gif) _a_<sub><i>k</i></sub>.

For example, 6 = 1 + 2 + 3 = 1 ![×](/Volumes/HDD_KS/source/project_euler/vender/bundle/ruby/2.2.0/gems/euler-manager-0.1.1/config/../data/images/symbol_times.gif) 2 ![×](/Volumes/HDD_KS/source/project_euler/vender/bundle/ruby/2.2.0/gems/euler-manager-0.1.1/config/../data/images/symbol_times.gif) 3.

For a given set of size, _k_, we shall call the smallest N with this property a minimal product-sum number. The minimal product-sum numbers for sets of size, _k_ = 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6 are as follows.

_k_=2: 4 = 2 ![×](/Volumes/HDD_KS/source/project_euler/vender/bundle/ruby/2.2.0/gems/euler-manager-0.1.1/config/../data/images/symbol_times.gif) 2 = 2 + 2  
_k_=3: 6 = 1 ![×](/Volumes/HDD_KS/source/project_euler/vender/bundle/ruby/2.2.0/gems/euler-manager-0.1.1/config/../data/images/symbol_times.gif) 2 ![×](/Volumes/HDD_KS/source/project_euler/vender/bundle/ruby/2.2.0/gems/euler-manager-0.1.1/config/../data/images/symbol_times.gif) 3 = 1 + 2 + 3  
_k_=4: 8 = 1 ![×](/Volumes/HDD_KS/source/project_euler/vender/bundle/ruby/2.2.0/gems/euler-manager-0.1.1/config/../data/images/symbol_times.gif) 1 ![×](/Volumes/HDD_KS/source/project_euler/vender/bundle/ruby/2.2.0/gems/euler-manager-0.1.1/config/../data/images/symbol_times.gif) 2 ![×](/Volumes/HDD_KS/source/project_euler/vender/bundle/ruby/2.2.0/gems/euler-manager-0.1.1/config/../data/images/symbol_times.gif) 4 = 1 + 1 + 2 + 4  
_k_=5: 8 = 1 ![×](/Volumes/HDD_KS/source/project_euler/vender/bundle/ruby/2.2.0/gems/euler-manager-0.1.1/config/../data/images/symbol_times.gif) 1 ![×](/Volumes/HDD_KS/source/project_euler/vender/bundle/ruby/2.2.0/gems/euler-manager-0.1.1/config/../data/images/symbol_times.gif) 2 ![×](/Volumes/HDD_KS/source/project_euler/vender/bundle/ruby/2.2.0/gems/euler-manager-0.1.1/config/../data/images/symbol_times.gif) 2 ![×](/Volumes/HDD_KS/source/project_euler/vender/bundle/ruby/2.2.0/gems/euler-manager-0.1.1/config/../data/images/symbol_times.gif) 2 = 1 + 1 + 2 + 2 + 2  
_k_=6: 12 = 1 ![×](/Volumes/HDD_KS/source/project_euler/vender/bundle/ruby/2.2.0/gems/euler-manager-0.1.1/config/../data/images/symbol_times.gif) 1 ![×](/Volumes/HDD_KS/source/project_euler/vender/bundle/ruby/2.2.0/gems/euler-manager-0.1.1/config/../data/images/symbol_times.gif) 1 ![×](/Volumes/HDD_KS/source/project_euler/vender/bundle/ruby/2.2.0/gems/euler-manager-0.1.1/config/../data/images/symbol_times.gif) 1 ![×](/Volumes/HDD_KS/source/project_euler/vender/bundle/ruby/2.2.0/gems/euler-manager-0.1.1/config/../data/images/symbol_times.gif) 2 ![×](/Volumes/HDD_KS/source/project_euler/vender/bundle/ruby/2.2.0/gems/euler-manager-0.1.1/config/../data/images/symbol_times.gif) 6 = 1 + 1 + 1 + 1 + 2 + 6

Hence for 2 ![≤](/Volumes/HDD_KS/source/project_euler/vender/bundle/ruby/2.2.0/gems/euler-manager-0.1.1/config/../data/images/symbol_le.gif)_k_ ![≤](/Volumes/HDD_KS/source/project_euler/vender/bundle/ruby/2.2.0/gems/euler-manager-0.1.1/config/../data/images/symbol_le.gif)6, the sum of all the minimal product-sum numbers is 4+6+8+12 = 30; note that 8 is only counted once in the sum.
